# TechAim Training Lab — Athlete & Coach Guide

*For version 0.9.0. Plain-language guide — no technical knowledge needed.*

## Demo vs Live
The badge at the top of every screen shows the mode. **LIVE TARGET** means
shots come from the physical TechAim target. **DEMO / SIMULATION** means you
click the on-screen target to simulate shots — **no physical target or COM
connection is needed in Demo**. Change mode in Settings ▸ Operating Mode or on
the start screen (requires an app restart). Demo results are always labelled
Demo and are never official.

## Open Practice
A flexible shooting session — pick a shot plan (10 / 20 / 30 / 40 / No limit)
on the Open Practice card and press *Start session*. Scores show normally.
It is not part of Training Lab and produces no blocks, reviews or notes.
*(Shot plans come from each discipline's event definitions, which is why 50 m
offers 20 / 40 / No limit.)*

## Technical Blocks — what and why
You shoot several **short groups (blocks)** while concentrating on **one
technical part** of your process (your *focus* — e.g. Trigger, Hold, Natural
point of aim). Short focused blocks with a review between them build
repeatability better than one long unfocused string. The focus is your
*intention* — TechAim never claims it caused any particular shot.

**Defaults:** Air Rifle 5×6 · Air Pistol 6×5 · Prone 5×6 · 3P 2 blocks of 6
per position (36). All configurable within safe limits.

## Visibility modes
- **Full hidden block** — no score and no impact while you shoot; you only see
  "n / m RECORDED". Everything is revealed at the block review.
- **Group only** — you see where shots land as a group, but no numbers.
- **Impact only** — you see each impact, but scores stay hidden.

Hiding results while shooting keeps your attention on the process, not the
outcome. Every shot is still fully measured and stored.

## Block review & notes
When a block's last shot is fired the block closes and the review opens
automatically: the revealed target, average score, group size (radius /
diameter), group centre (MPI), spread and timing. You may type **one note per
block** ("felt rushed", "good NPA") — press *Save Note*, then *Continue* to
start the next block, or *End Training* to stop early (finished blocks are
kept; nothing is invented for unfinished ones).

## Three Positions
3P keeps **Kneeling, Prone and Standing separate** — the plan runs
**K1 → K2 → P1 → P2 → S1 → S2** (with 2 blocks per position by default).
Each position gets its own measurements, notes and comparison; results are
never merged into one generic 3P analysis.

## Session summary
After the last block: a **TRAINING SESSION** summary ("Not an official
competition result") comparing every block — best score, tightest group, most
repeatable, group-size change and centre drift — plus your notes. *New
Session* starts fresh (the old session is preserved); *Home* returns to the
start screen.

## If the app crashes
Training sessions are saved shot-by-shot. On restart TechAim offers
**TRAINING SESSION · Technical Blocks** recovery — *Resume* returns you
exactly where you were (mid-block, review, or the finished summary), with
hidden scores still hidden. A training session is never confused with a
competition match.

## Training vs competition
Training Lab results are for development only: no rankings, placements or
official scores, and they never appear in competition records or match
recovery. Official ISSF events live in their own section on the start screen.
